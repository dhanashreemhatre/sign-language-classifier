Sign Language

Sign languages play an important role in the deaf community, enabling individuals to communicate effectively and participate fully in social, educational, and professional settings. They are also valued for their unique cultural contributions and the unique perspectives they offer on the nature of language and communication.

They are also valued for their unique cultural contributions and the unique perspectives they offer on the nature of language and communication.
It is important to note that sign language is not a universal language and is not equivalent to gestures or mime. Each sign language is a complete, standalone language in its own right, with its own linguistic rules and structure.
